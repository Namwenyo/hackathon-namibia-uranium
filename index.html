About Namibia's Uranium Resources

Namibia is one of the world's top uranium producers, accounting for approximately 10-11% of global uranium output. The country's uranium deposits are primarily located in the Erongo region and central Namib Desert.

There are two main geological settings for uranium in Namibia:

  • Late-tectonic alaskite (granitic) intrusions in the Damara orogen (e.g., the Rossing and Husab deposits)
  • Surficial calcrete/paleochannel deposits in the Namib Desert (e.g., Langer Heinrich, Trekkopje)

The uranium sector is crucial to Namibia's economy, with strict regulations under the Minerals Act, Environmental Management Act, and Atomic Energy Act.
